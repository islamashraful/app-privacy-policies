No accounts, no ads, no personal tracking — anonymous analytics and crash reporting only

This Privacy Policy explains how Pause ("the app"), published by Ashraful Islam, handles information. Pause is designed to help you wait before buying non-essential items. The app is built around local, on-device storage and does not require an account.

1. Information We Collect

1a. Data you enter — stored locally on your device

Pause does not require an account and does not ask for your name, email address, phone number, payment information, or location. Everything you type into the app stays on your device in a local database. This includes:

  • Item names you add to your waiting list
  • Optional prices and notes you choose to enter
  • Item statuses — waiting, ready for review, bought, skipped, or bypassed
  • Dates used by the app, such as when an item was created, when it becomes ready for review, and when a decision was made
  • App settings, including waiting period, currency, language, onboarding state, and notification preference

None of this content is transmitted to Ashraful Islam's servers or shared with third parties.

1b. Anonymous device identifier

The app generates a random anonymous identifier (a UUID prefixed with pause_) the first time it launches and stores it in the local database. This identifier is not linked to your Apple ID, name, email, or any other personal information. It is used solely to associate anonymous analytics events from the same device so aggregate product behaviour can be understood (see Section 5).

1c. Diagnostic and crash data

When a crash or unhandled error occurs, technical diagnostic information is sent to Sentry (see Section 6). This may include the type of error, a stack trace, the app version, and device/OS type. Sentry does not receive your item names, notes, prices, or any other content you entered into Pause.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

  • No user accounts or authentication credentials
  • No name, email address, or phone number
  • No advertising identifiers (IDFA or equivalent)
  • No cross-app or cross-website tracking
  • No precise or approximate location data
  • No contacts, photos, camera, or microphone data
  • No item names, notes, or prices are sent to any server

3. How Information Is Used

The information stored on your device is used only to provide the app's core features: keeping your waiting list, calculating review dates, showing your decision history, formatting prices, remembering your settings, and optionally scheduling local reminders.

The anonymous identifier and aggregate usage events are used solely to understand how people use the app in general — for example, which features are most used or at what point users complete onboarding — so that the app can be improved. This data is never used for advertising, profiling, or sold to third parties.

Crash data is used only to identify and fix software bugs.

4. Local Storage

Pause stores all user-entered data in a local SQLite database on your device using expo-sqlite. Ashraful Islam does not operate a user account system for Pause and does not store your waiting-list items on remote servers.

Because your app data is local to your device, it may be included in device backups (e.g. iCloud Backup or Android cloud backup) depending on your operating system and backup settings.

5. Notifications

Notifications are optional and require your explicit permission. If you enable them, Pause schedules local reminders on your device to notify you when an item is ready for review. The notification title and body may include the item name so the reminder is meaningful to you.

Pause uses only local scheduled notifications. It does not register a remote push notification token with Ashraful Islam and does not transmit your item list to any notification server.

6. Third-Party Services

PostHog — Anonymous product analytics

Pause uses PostHog to collect anonymous, aggregate product analytics. PostHog's servers are hosted in the EU (eu.i.posthog.com). The data sent to PostHog includes:

  • The anonymous device identifier described in Section 1b
  • App version, platform (iOS/Android), and environment
  • Device language and selected currency (e.g. "USD") — not your identity
  • App lifecycle events (app opened, backgrounded)
  • Feature interaction events such as "item added", "item decision made", "onboarding step completed", "settings changed", or "notification permission outcome" — described using bucketed or enumerated values, never free text you typed
  • Screen navigation paths — these use route identifiers, not your item names or content

PostHog is configured with session replay and touch/screen autocapture disabled. Your item names, notes, prices, and any text you enter are not sent to PostHog.

PostHog's privacy policy: posthog.com/privacy

Sentry — Crash and error reporting

Pause uses Sentry for crash and error reporting in the production version of the app. When the app crashes or encounters an unhandled error, Sentry receives:

  • Error type and stack trace
  • App version and build number
  • Device type and operating system version
  • A trace sample (approximately 20% of sessions) for performance monitoring

Sentry does not receive your item names, notes, prices, or decision history. Crash reporting is disabled in development builds and is only active in the publicly distributed version.

Sentry's privacy policy: sentry.io/privacy

Expo / EAS Update — App updates

Pause uses Expo's over-the-air (OTA) update technology to deliver app updates. Update checks contact Expo's servers and involve standard technical metadata such as app version and update channel. Your item names, notes, prices, and decision history are not transmitted as part of this process.

Expo's privacy policy: expo.dev/privacy

Apple App Store version check

To inform you of available updates, Pause queries Apple's public iTunes Search API using the app's bundle identifier (com.ashislam.pause) to compare the installed version with the latest version on the App Store. This request does not include any user content.

7. Data Sharing and Sale

Ashraful Islam does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party. The only data shared with third parties is the anonymous analytics data described in Section 6 (PostHog) and the technical crash/diagnostic data (Sentry), neither of which includes your item content or identifies you personally.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

Your Pause data (items, settings, and the anonymous device identifier) remains on your device until you delete items, reset the relevant device storage, or uninstall the app. Uninstalling Pause removes the app's locally stored data from your device, subject to your operating system and backup behaviour.

Because Pause does not store your item content on Ashraful Islam's servers, there is no remote account data to request deletion of.

Anonymous analytics events stored by PostHog are retained according to PostHog's data retention policy. These events are not linked to any personally identifiable information.

9. Children's Privacy

Pause is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information through the app, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

10. Your Rights

Because Pause does not collect personally identifiable information and all item data is stored locally on your device, most data subject rights (access, correction, portability, deletion) can be exercised directly within the app or by uninstalling it.

For questions about the anonymous analytics data held by PostHog, you may contact us at the address below with your anonymous device identifier (visible in the app's settings if applicable) and we will assist with any applicable request.

11. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes in the app's features or applicable law. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. Continued use of the app after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
